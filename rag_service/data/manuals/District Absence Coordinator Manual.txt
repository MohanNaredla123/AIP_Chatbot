1. Introduction  
    1.1 About AIP  
      The Attendance Improvement Plan (AIP) is an online system that helps New Mexico schools to create student  
      attendance plan and create action plans to improve it.  
      • Track attendance trends across all district schools.  
      • Plan district‑wide strategies to improve attendance.  
      • Monitor progress through reporting periods.

    1.2 Your District Responsibilities  
      ✔ Submit an annual district attendance improvement plan  
      ✔ Set targets for all schools and student subgroups  
      ✔ Monitor school plan implementation  
      ✔ Update district progress after every reporting period  

2. Getting Started  
   2.1 How to Register  
      1) Click "Register New Account"  
      2) Fill Out:  
         o First Name / Last Name  
         o District Email (e.g., district.nm.gov; personal emails like Gmail are blocked).  
         o District Name (select from dropdown).  
         o Phone Number  
         o Password Requirements:  
            a. 12+ characters  
            b. 1 uppercase, 1 number, 1 special character (!, @, #, etc.).  
            c. Expires every 60 days.  
         o Role: Select District Absence Coordinator.  
      3) Submit: Your request will be approved by PED Absence Coordinator.  
   2.2 Registration and Approval Workflow
      Registration & Approval Process
         - Start
         - Fill Out Form
         - Check: Is Email Domain Valid?
            - If No
               - Show Error
               - End
            - If Yes
               - Send Request to Approver
               - Review Request
               - Check: Approve Registration?
                     - If No
                        - Notify Coordinator
                        - End
                     - If Yes
                        - Activate Account
                        - Send Confirmation Email
                        - End
   2.3 Logging In  
      1) Visit Application  
      2) Enter Credentials:  
         o Registered email  
         o Password  
      3) Two-Step Authentication:  
         o A one‑time code (OTP) will be sent to your email. Enter it to proceed.
   2.4 Login Workflow
      Login Process
         - User Attempts Login
         - User Enters Email & Password
         - Check: Is Email Registered?
            - If No
               - Account not found
               - End
            - If Yes
               - Send Security Access Code to Email
               - User Enters Security Access Code
               - Check: Is Security Access Code Valid?
                     - If No
                        - Invalid Code
                        - End
                     - If Yes
                        - Access Granted
                        - End
   2.5 Key Features and Navigation  
      • Landing Page: If you have multiple roles (e.g., School + District Coordinator), select your role.  
      • Home Screen: Displays certified schools/districts for the fiscal year.  
      • Announcements: Check for PED alerts (e.g., deadlines, policy updates).  


3. Creating Your District Plan
    3.1 Steps to complete Attendance Plan:
         Follow these 8 steps to complete your district level attendance Plan:
            District Attendance Plan Creation Process
               1) District Contact Information – Enter district and contact details
               2) Subpopulation Targets – Define targets for specific student groups
               3) School Targets – Set targets for absence rate improvement
               4) Tiers of Support – Implement strategies for different support levels
               5) Upload Documents – Upload necessary reports and documents
               6) Final Submittal – Submit the completed attendance plan
               7) Progress Updates – Monitor and report on progress at intervals
               8) Print/Export – Generate and export the final report


    3.2 District Contact Information

        Purpose: Identify the primary contact for the attendance plan and add team members involved in the process.
        Steps:
        1) School Contact:
            o Enter the contact person’s first name, last name, title, and email.
            o Example:
                LEA Name: BELEN CONSOLIDATED SCHOOLS
                School Name: Kristin
                Contact First Name: Amanda
                Contact Last Name: Whitmore
                Contact Email: Whitmore@teach.com

        2) Save Progress – Click Save before navigating to the next tab.

    3.3 Step 2 – School Targets

        Purpose: Enable District Absence Coordinator to set and monitor attendance‑improvement targets for their district and subsequent schools.

        How to Access:
            Navigate to Home > [Your District] > School Improvement Targets

    3.4 Step 3 – Subpopulation Improvement Targets for District Plan

        Purpose: Identify attendance disparities across student groups to target interventions effectively.
        
        How to Access:
            Navigate to Home > [Your District] > Subpopulation Improvement Targets

        Two sections appear in Subpopulation Improvement Targets:
            a) District Attendance Improvement Targets by Subpopulation
            b) District Grade Improvement Targets by Sub‑population

            A) District Attendance Improvement Targets by subpopulation:
                • Subpopulation Name: Auto‑filled groups (e.g., Female, Economically‑Disadvantaged)
                • Current Year Enrollment Number: Total students in subgroup (auto‑pulled from backend)
                • Historical Chronic Absence Rates: Historical chronic‑absence rates (read‑only)
                • Current Year Attendance Improvement Target: Enter your Goal (%) to reduce chronic absence for each subgroup

            B) District Grade Improvement Targets
                • Subpopulation Grade Level: Pre‑K through 12th grade (auto‑filled)
                • Current Year Enrollment Number: Total students per grade (auto‑pulled)
                • Historical Chronic Absence Rates: Past chronic‑absence rates (read‑only)
                • Current Year Attendance Improvement Target: Set Grade‑specific improvement goals

    3.5 Tiers of Support

        Purpose: Develop targeted interventions based on chronic absence severity. Each tier corresponds to a student's absenteeism level and requires specific strategies.

        1) Tier 1 (0 – 5 % absence rate) – Whole School Prevention
            Focus: District-wide programs to promote attendance for all students.
            Steps to Complete:
                A. Add Strategy
                     o List your preferred Strategy.  
                     o Provide a description of your strategy 
                     o Outline your strategy based on determination from the root cause analysis, key progress indicators, data analysis, etc.
                B. Implementation Plan
                    o Action Steps: List specific activities.
                    o Responsible Parties: Assign staff.
                    o Resources Needed: Budget, materials.
                    o Timeframe: Set milestones.

        2) Tier 2 (5 – 10 % absence) – Individualized Prevention
            Focus: Targeted support for students at risk of chronic absence.
            Steps to Complete:
                A. Add Strategy
                     o List your preferred Strategy.  
                     o Provide a description of your strategy 
                     o Outline your strategy based on determination from the root cause analysis, key progress indicators, data analysis, etc.
                B. Implementation Plan
                    o Action Steps: "Pair at‑risk students with peer mentors."
                    o Responsible Parties: "Mentor Coordinator: John Doe."
                    o Resources: "Training materials for mentors."
                    o Timeline: "Match mentors by Week 6."

        3) Tier 3 (10 – 20 % absence) – Early Intervention
            Focus: Address emerging chronic absence with structured support.
            Steps to Complete:
                A. Add Strategy
                     o List your preferred Strategy.  
                     o Provide a description of your strategy 
                     o Outline your strategy based on determination from the root cause analysis, key progress indicators, data analysis, etc.
                B. Implementation Plan
                    o Action Steps: "Schedule home visits for students missing > 15 %."
                    o Responsible Parties: "Social Worker: Lee Chen."
                    o Resources: "Transportation for home visits."
                    o Timeline: "Complete visits by November 30."
                
        4) Tier 4 (20 %+ absence) – Intensive Support
            Focus: High‑intensity interventions for severe chronic absence.
            Steps to Complete:
                A. Add Strategy
                     o List your preferred Strategy.  
                     o Provide a description of your strategy 
                     o Outline your strategy based on determination from the root cause analysis, key progress indicators, data analysis, etc.
                B. Implementation Plan
                   o Action Steps: "Coordinate with local clinics for health barriers."
                   o Responsible Parties: "Nurse: Sarah Kim."
                   o Resources: "Memorandum of Understanding with Clinic X."
                   o Timeline: "Sign MOU by September 1"

    3.6 Final Submittal
        Steps:
            a) Review all entered data.
            b) Check the certification box: "I confirm that my submission is complete and final."
            c) Click Submit Plan.

    3.7 Upload Documents

        Purpose: District Absence Coordinator will be able to upload Attendance Plan Review documents for the school. 

    3.8 Progress Updates
        Reporting Periods: 80‑day, 120‑day, End of School Year
        Steps:
            a) Current chronic‑absence rates.
            b) Reflect on strategy effectiveness.
            c) Adjust targets for the next period.

    3.9 Print Your Plan
        Options:
            a) Full PDF Report: Includes all sections and charts.
            b) Custom Reports: Select specific sections for sharing.
        Steps:
            a) Click Print Plan.
            b) Choose format and generate.

      ✔ Update progress after every reporting period and adjust targets.

6. Creating Your School Plan
   6.1 Steps to complete your school level attendance plan:
         Follow these 7 steps to complete your school‑level AIP. 
         School Attendance Improvement Plan Flow:
            1) Contact Information – Identify primary contact
            2) Subpopulation Targets – Set improvement goals
            3) Root Cause – Analyze core issues
            4) Tiers of Support – Implement tiered strategies
            5) Progress Updates – Report on progress
            6) Final Submittal – Submit plan for review
            7) Print Plan – Finalize and print
   
   6.2 Contact Information  
      Purpose: Identify the primary contact for the attendance plan and add team members involved in the process.
 
      Steps:
         1) School Contact:
            o Enter the contact person’s first name, last name, title, and email.
            o Example:
               LEA Name: BELEN CONSOLIDATED SCHOOLS  
               School Name: BELEN HIGH  
               Contact First Name: Kristin  
               Contact Last Name: Garcia  
               Contact Email: garcia@tech.com
 
         2) Save Progress – Click Save before navigating to the next tab.  
 
   6.3 Subpopulation Targets  
      Purpose: Identify attendance disparities across student groups to target interventions effectively.  
      How to Access:  
         1) Navigate to "Home > [Your School] > Subpopulation Improvement Targets".  
         2) Two sections appear:  
            a) School Attendance Improvement Targets by Subpopulation   
            b) School Grade Attendance Improvement Targets by Subpopulation
 
      A. School Attendance Improvement Targets by Subpopulation  
         Subpopulation Name: Auto‑filled groups (e.g., Female, Economically‑Disadvantaged)  
         Current Year Enrollment: Total students in subgroup (auto pulled from backend) 
         Historical Rates: Historical chronic absence rates (grayed out; read-only) 
         Current Year Target: Enter your goal (%) to reduce chronic absence for each subgroup
 
      B. School Grade Attendance Targets by Subpopulation
         Grade Level: Pre-K through 12th grade (auto-filled)  
         Current Year Enrollment: Total students per grade (auto-pulled)  
         Historical Rates: Past chronic absence rates (read-only)  
         Current Year Target: Set grade-specific improvement goals
 
   6.4 Attendance Improvement Plan Guidelines
      Purpose: 
         The guideline structure ensures that schools follow a consistent, data-informed approach to reduce chronic 
         absenteeism through tiered interventions. 
      Step to Complete: 
          Step 1: Determine Stakeholder Needs and Challenges 
          Step 2: Get to the Core Issues Using the Root Cause Analysis 
          Step 3: Develop Targeted Strategies 
          Step 4: Measure Progress (every 40 days) 
          Step 5: Review Progress Using Continuous Quality Improvement
